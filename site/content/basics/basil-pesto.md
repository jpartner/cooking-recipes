# Basil Pesto

Classic Genovese pesto — basil, pine nuts, parmesan, garlic, and good olive oil. Makes about 200ml, enough for 4 portions of pasta.

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Fresh basil leaves, packed | 60g (~2 large bunches) |
| Pine nuts | 40g |
| Parmesan, finely grated | 50g |
| Pecorino Romano, finely grated | 20g (optional — or use all parmesan) |
| Garlic | 1–2 cloves |
| Extra virgin olive oil | 100–120ml |
| Lemon juice | 1 tsp |
| Salt | to taste |

## Method

### 1. Toast the pine nuts

Dry-toast in a small pan over medium-low heat, shaking frequently, until evenly golden — 3–4 minutes. Watch them constantly — they burn in seconds. Tip onto a plate to cool.

### 2. Pound or process

**Pestle and mortar (best texture):** start with the garlic and a pinch of salt — grind to a paste. Add the pine nuts and crush to a rough paste. Add the basil in handfuls, grinding and pressing until broken down. Stir in the cheese, then drizzle in the olive oil gradually, mixing until you reach the consistency you want.

**Food processor (faster):** pulse the garlic and pine nuts until roughly chopped. Add the basil and pulse in short bursts — don't let the motor run or the heat will bruise the basil and turn it dark. Add the cheese, pulse briefly, then stream in the olive oil with the motor running on low.

### 3. Season

Add lemon juice and salt to taste. The cheese is already salty so taste before adding much salt.

## Serving

- **With pasta:** toss with hot pasta and a splash of the starchy pasta water — the starch emulsifies the oil and makes it coat properly. Never heat the pesto itself.
- **On bruschetta:** spread on toasted bread with fresh tomatoes.
- **With risotto:** stir through at the very end, off the heat.
- **As a marinade:** thin with extra oil for chicken or fish.

## Notes

- **Basil:** use at room temperature, not cold from the fridge. Wash and dry thoroughly — any water dilutes the pesto and shortens shelf life.
- **Pine nuts:** toasting is not optional. Raw pine nuts taste flat and waxy. Toasted ones are sweet and nutty — it's a completely different ingredient.
- **Colour:** pesto darkens quickly once made. A squeeze of lemon helps slow this. If storing, press cling film directly onto the surface or pour a thin layer of olive oil over the top.
- **Don't over-process.** You want some texture — a rough, grainy paste, not a smooth puree.
- **Keeps:** 5–7 days refrigerated with oil on the surface. Freezes well in ice cube trays for up to 3 months.
- **Cost of pine nuts:** if they're expensive, swap half for blanched almonds or walnuts. Not traditional but still good.
