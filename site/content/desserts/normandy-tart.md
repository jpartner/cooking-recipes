# Normandy Apple Tart

Apple and frangipane tart on a shortcrust base — a Prue Leith classic. The frangipane puffs up around the apples and sets into a soft almond sponge. Serves 8–10.

## Shortcrust pastry

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Plain flour | 225g |
| Cold butter, cubed | 110g |
| Caster sugar | 30g |
| Egg yolk | 1 |
| Cold water | 2–3 tbsp |
| Pinch of salt | |

### Method

1. Pulse flour, salt, sugar, and cold butter in a food processor until it resembles breadcrumbs. Or rub in by hand.
2. Add the egg yolk and 2 tbsp cold water. Pulse (or mix with a knife) until it just comes together. Add the third tablespoon of water only if needed — the dough should hold when pressed but not be wet.
3. Flatten into a disc, wrap in cling film, and refrigerate for **at least 30 minutes**.
4. Roll out on a floured surface to ~3mm thick. Line a 25cm loose-bottomed tart tin, pressing gently into the corners. Leave a slight overhang — trim after blind baking.
5. Prick the base with a fork, line with parchment and baking beans. **Blind bake** at 190°C fan for 15 minutes, remove the beans and parchment, then bake for a further 5 minutes until the base is dry and pale gold.
6. Trim the overhanging pastry with a sharp knife while still warm.

## Frangipane

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Butter, softened | 125g |
| Caster sugar | 125g |
| Ground almonds | 125g |
| Eggs | 2 |
| Plain flour | 1 tbsp |
| Almond extract | 1/2 tsp |
| Vanilla extract | 1 tsp |

### Method

1. Beat the softened butter and sugar together until light and fluffy — 3–4 minutes with an electric mixer.
2. Beat in the eggs one at a time, adding a spoonful of ground almonds with each to prevent curdling.
3. Fold in the remaining ground almonds, flour, almond extract, and vanilla. The mixture should be thick and spreadable.

## Apples

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Eating apples (Cox, Braeburn, or similar) | 4–5 medium |
| Lemon juice | 1 tbsp |
| Caster sugar | 1 tbsp |

Peel, core, and cut into thin slices (~3mm). Toss with lemon juice to prevent browning.

## Assembly and bake

1. Spread the frangipane evenly into the blind-baked tart case.
2. Arrange the apple slices over the frangipane — either in concentric circles for a classic look, or scatter them in if you prefer. Press them in slightly but don't submerge them — the frangipane will puff up and hold them.
3. Sprinkle 1 tbsp caster sugar over the apples.
4. Bake at **170°C fan** for **40–45 minutes** until the frangipane is puffed, golden, and set. A skewer into the centre should come out clean.
5. Cool in the tin for 15 minutes, then remove and cool on a wire rack.

## Glaze

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Apricot jam | 3 tbsp |
| Water | 1 tbsp |

Warm the apricot jam with 1 tbsp water in a small pan, sieve to remove lumps, then brush over the warm tart. This gives the classic glossy finish.

## Serve

Warm or at room temperature. Excellent with creme fraiche, double cream, or the salted caramel ice cream.

## Notes

- **Pastry:** keep everything cold. If the butter softens too much, the pastry will be tough. If it cracks when rolling, let it warm up for 2–3 minutes — too cold is better than too warm.
- **Blind baking** is essential. Without it the base will be soggy under the frangipane. The base should be dry and firm before you fill it.
- **Apples:** use eating apples, not cooking apples. Bramleys collapse into mush — you want slices that hold their shape. Cox or Braeburn work well.
- **Frangipane ratio:** the classic is equal weights of butter, sugar, and almonds. Easy to scale — 100g of each for a smaller tart, 150g for a deeper one.
- **Calvados (optional):** for a properly Normandy version, add 2 tbsp Calvados to the frangipane and brush the blind-baked pastry base with another tablespoon before filling.
- **Keeps:** 2–3 days at room temperature, loosely covered. Reheat gently in a low oven to refresh.
