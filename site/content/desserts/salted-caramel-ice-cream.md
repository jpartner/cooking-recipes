# Salted Caramel Ice Cream

A proper custard-based salted caramel ice cream — deep, bittersweet caramel with a good hit of salt. Makes about 1 litre.

## Ingredients

### Caramel

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Caster sugar | 200g |
| Double cream | 250ml |
| Flaky sea salt (Maldon or similar) | 1 tsp + extra to finish |

### Custard

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Whole milk | 250ml |
| Double cream | 200ml |
| Egg yolks | 6 |
| Caster sugar | 50g |
| Vanilla extract | 1 tsp |

## Method

### 1. Make the caramel

Put 200g caster sugar in a heavy-bottomed saucepan over medium heat. **Don't stir** — just swirl the pan occasionally. The sugar will melt unevenly, then gradually turn amber. Watch it carefully once it starts to colour — it goes from perfect to burnt in seconds.

When it reaches a **deep amber** (darker than you think — pale caramel tastes bland), remove from the heat and carefully pour in 250ml double cream. It will bubble up violently — stand back and whisk through it. Return to low heat and stir until smooth. Add 1 tsp flaky salt. Set aside.

### 2. Make the custard base

Whisk 6 egg yolks with 50g caster sugar until pale and slightly thick.

In a separate saucepan, heat 250ml milk and 200ml double cream until just steaming — not boiling.

Pour the hot milk mixture slowly into the egg yolks, whisking constantly. Return everything to the pan over low heat and cook, stirring with a spatula, until the custard coats the back of a spoon and reads **82–84°C** on a thermometer. Don't let it boil or it will scramble.

### 3. Combine

Pour the warm caramel into the custard. Add 1 tsp vanilla extract. Whisk until fully combined. Taste — it should be noticeably salty and sweet. Add more salt if needed; it dulls when frozen so season a little more than you think.

### 4. Chill

Strain through a fine sieve into a bowl. Press cling film directly onto the surface (prevents a skin). Refrigerate for **at least 4 hours**, ideally overnight. The colder the base, the better it churns.

### 5. Churn

Pour into your ice cream maker and churn according to the machine's instructions — usually 25–35 minutes. It's done when it holds soft-serve consistency.

Transfer to a freezer-safe container, press cling film onto the surface, and freeze for **at least 4 hours** to firm up.

### 6. Serve

Remove from the freezer 10–15 minutes before serving — proper ice cream needs to temper. Finish with a small pinch of flaky salt on top.

## Notes

- **Caramel darkness:** take it darker than feels comfortable. Light caramel tastes like sweet nothing once frozen. You want it just short of smoking — that bittersweet edge is the whole point.
- **No ice cream maker?** Pour the chilled base into a shallow container and freeze. Whisk vigorously (or blitz in a food processor) every 45 minutes for 3–4 hours until set. Won't be as smooth but still very good.
- **Salt:** flaky salt on top at serving is non-negotiable. It lifts the whole thing. Fine table salt dissolves and disappears — you want the crunch.
- **Egg whites:** save them for meringues or freeze for later.
- **Keeps:** 2 weeks in the freezer before ice crystals start to develop.
