# Smoky Hummus — From Dried Chickpeas

Proper hummus from dried chickpeas with a smoky paprika and chilli kick. Dramatically better than anything from a tin — smoother, more flavourful, and worth the overnight soak.

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Dried chickpeas | 250g (~600g cooked) |
| Bicarbonate of soda | 1 tsp (for soaking) + 1/2 tsp (for cooking) |
| Tahini (good quality, runny) | 120g |
| Lemon juice | 60ml (~2 lemons) |
| Garlic, minced | 2–3 cloves |
| Ice-cold water | 60–80ml |
| Smoked paprika | 1.5 tsp |
| Cayenne or Aleppo chilli flakes | 1/2–1 tsp (to taste) |
| Ground cumin | 1 tsp |
| Salt | 1 tsp (adjust to taste) |
| Extra virgin olive oil | for finishing |

## Method

### 1. Soak (overnight)

Cover dried chickpeas with plenty of cold water — they'll double in size. Add 1 tsp bicarbonate of soda. Soak **12–24 hours**.

### 2. Cook (45–60 min)

Drain and rinse. Add to a large pot, cover generously with fresh water, add 1/2 tsp bicarbonate of soda. Bring to a boil, skim off any foam and loose skins that float to the surface.

Simmer until the chickpeas are **very soft** — they should crush easily between two fingers with almost no resistance. This is the single most important step. Under-cooked chickpeas = grainy hummus. 45–60 minutes typically, but test by feel, not time.

### 3. Skin (optional but worth it)

For the smoothest result, rub the cooked chickpeas gently in a bowl of water — the skins slip off and float to the surface. Skim them off. This takes 5 minutes and makes a real difference.

### 4. Blend the tahini base first

Process the tahini, lemon juice, garlic, smoked paprika, cayenne/chilli, cumin, and salt in a food processor for **2 minutes** until light and whipped. This step is key — blending tahini with the acid first creates a smoother emulsion.

### 5. Add chickpeas and blend

Add the warm, drained chickpeas. Process for **3–4 minutes**, scraping down the sides halfway through. Stream in ice-cold water until you reach a smooth, creamy consistency. The hummus should be noticeably smoother than the chickpeas-first approach.

Taste and adjust — more lemon, salt, or chilli as needed.

### 6. Serve

Spread onto a plate with the back of a spoon, creating a swirl. Finish with:

- A generous drizzle of extra virgin olive oil
- A dusting of smoked paprika
- A pinch of chilli flakes
- Optional: whole cooked chickpeas, fresh parsley, toasted pine nuts

## Notes

- **Dried vs tinned:** dried chickpeas cooked soft produce a dramatically smoother hummus. Tinned works in a pinch — rinse well and simmer for 20 minutes with bicarb to soften further.
- **Tahini quality matters.** Look for tahini that pours — if it's stiff and paste-like, the hummus will be heavy. Ingredients should be just sesame seeds.
- **Ice-cold water** while blending helps emulsify and lighten the texture.
- **Heat level:** 1/2 tsp cayenne gives a gentle warmth. 1 tsp gives noticeable heat. Aleppo chilli is milder and fruitier if you prefer that direction.
- **Keeps:** 4–5 days refrigerated. Drizzle oil over the surface before storing to prevent drying out.
- **Scales linearly** — double everything for a party batch.
