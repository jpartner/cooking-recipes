# Nut & Berry Granola — 2kg Batch

Scaled for a full 2kg batch, vac-packed into 4x500g bags. Bake on two separate trays for best results.

## Ingredients

### Dry

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Rolled oats (platki owsiane gorskie) | 700g |
| Mixed nuts — almonds, cashews, walnuts, hazelnuts | 350g |
| Pumpkin seeds | 120g |
| Desiccated coconut (wiorki kokosowe) | 95g |

### Wet

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Rapeseed oil | 130g |
| Honey | 185g |
| Vanilla extract | 9g |
| Cinnamon | 7g |
| Salt | 7g |

### Add after baking

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Dried cranberries (zurawina) | 140g |
| Dried sour cherries or raisins | 140g |
| Dried mango or apricots, roughly chopped | 120g |

## Method

1. **Preheat oven** to 160°C fan. Line two large baking trays with parchment. At this batch size you must use two trays — one tray will be too thick to bake evenly and you won't get good clusters.

2. **Chop the nuts.** Roughly chop the mixed nuts — you want a mix of halves and smaller pieces, not a uniform fine chop. Texture variety is important.

3. **Mix the wet ingredients.** Whisk together the rapeseed oil, honey, vanilla, cinnamon, and salt until fully combined. No need to heat — rapeseed oil is already liquid.

4. **Combine the dry ingredients.** In a very large bowl (or split across two bowls) combine the oats, nuts, pumpkin seeds, and coconut. Pour over the wet mixture and toss very thoroughly — every oat should be lightly coated.

5. **Divide and press.** Split evenly between the two lined trays. Press down firmly with the back of a spatula — this is the cluster secret. Aim for an even layer roughly 1.5–2cm thick.

6. **Bake.** 35–45 minutes at 160°C fan, rotating trays and swapping shelves at the halfway point. Check the back corners around the 25-minute mark — granola browns fast at the edges. Pull when uniformly golden.

7. **Cool undisturbed.** Leave the trays completely alone until fully cool. Do not stir, do not break up. This is what gives you clusters.

8. **Add the dried fruit** once cool, then break into clusters of your preferred size.

9. **Vac-pack** into 4x500g bags.

## Notes

- **Shelf life vac-packed:** 3–4 months at room temperature, 6+ months frozen.
- **Sweetness:** 185g honey gives a lightly sweet result. Drop to 140g for less sweet, adding an extra 20g oil to maintain binding.
- **Clusters:** the longer you leave it undisturbed after baking, the better. Don't rush the cooling step.
- **Hot spots:** check back corners of each tray around the 25-minute mark — granola browns fast at the edges.
