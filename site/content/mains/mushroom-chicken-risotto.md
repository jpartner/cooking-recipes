# Mushroom & Chicken Risotto

Rich risotto with dried ceps, fresh button mushrooms, chicken breast, white wine, and a hit of truffle paste. Serves 4.

## Ingredients

### Cep stock

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Dried ceps (porcini) | 30g |
| Boiling water | 400ml |

Soak the ceps in 400ml boiling water for **at least 30 minutes**. Strain through a fine sieve or muslin — the soaking liquid is intensely flavoured and becomes part of the stock. Roughly chop the rehydrated ceps. Reserve both separately.

### Main

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Chicken breast | 2 (~400g) |
| Button mushrooms, sliced | 300g |
| Risotto rice (arborio or carnaroli) | 320g |
| Dry white wine | 150ml |
| Chicken stock (warm) | 800ml |
| Cep soaking liquid (from above) | ~400ml |
| Onion, finely diced | 1 large |
| Garlic, minced | 3 cloves |
| Truffle paste | 2–3 tsp (to taste) |
| Butter | 40g |
| Parmesan, finely grated | 60g |
| Olive oil | 2 tbsp |
| Salt and black pepper | to taste |
| Fresh parsley, chopped | for finishing |

## Method

### 1. Prep the stock

Combine the warm chicken stock and strained cep soaking liquid in a saucepan. Keep on a low heat — the stock must be hot when it goes into the rice.

### 2. Cook the chicken

**Pan method:** Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Sear for 4–5 minutes per side until golden and cooked through. Rest for 5 minutes, then slice or shred into bite-sized pieces. Set aside.

**Sous vide method (recommended):** Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper, vacuum seal with a sprig of thyme. Cook at **62°C for 1 hour**. Can be done well ahead — chill the sealed bag in ice water and refrigerate until needed. Slice or shred before folding into the risotto. Optionally sear briefly in a hot pan after sous vide for colour and fond — 30 seconds per side is enough.

### 3. Cook the mushrooms

In the same pan (use the fond from searing if you have it), add the sliced button mushrooms and the chopped rehydrated ceps. Cook over high heat for 5–6 minutes until golden and any moisture has evaporated. Don't crowd the pan — you want colour, not steam. Season lightly and set aside with the chicken.

### 4. Build the risotto base

In a wide, heavy-bottomed pan, melt half the butter with 1 tbsp olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and cook for 5–6 minutes until soft and translucent. Add the garlic, cook for 1 minute.

### 5. Toast the rice

Add the rice and stir for 1–2 minutes until the grains are hot, coated in fat, and slightly translucent at the edges. You should hear a gentle crackle.

### 6. Deglaze with wine

Pour in the white wine. Stir continuously until it's fully absorbed. This is where the acidity and flavour base comes from — don't skip it.

### 7. Build (18–20 minutes)

Add the hot stock **one ladleful at a time**, stirring frequently. Wait until each addition is mostly absorbed before adding the next. Keep the heat at a steady simmer — not a boil, not a crawl.

The rice is done when it's creamy but still has a slight bite at the centre. You may not need all the stock, or you may need a splash more — judge by texture, not volume.

### 8. Finish

Take the pan off the heat. Stir in:

1. The remaining butter and the parmesan — stir vigorously for 30 seconds. This is the *mantecatura* — it creates the creamy, glossy texture.
2. The truffle paste — 2 tsp to start, taste, add more if you want.
3. The mushrooms and chicken — fold in gently to warm through.

Season with salt and pepper. The risotto should flow lazily across the plate — if it holds its shape like a mound, it's too thick. Loosen with a small splash of hot stock.

### 9. Serve

Plate immediately — risotto waits for no one. Finish with chopped parsley, a crack of black pepper, and extra parmesan if you like.

## Notes

- **Carnaroli vs arborio:** carnaroli holds its shape better and is more forgiving. Arborio works fine but watch it more closely toward the end.
- **Truffle paste:** a little goes a long way. Start with 2 tsp, taste, then adjust. It should be a background note, not the dominant flavour.
- **Cep soaking liquid** is the secret weapon — it adds a depth of mushroom flavour that fresh mushrooms alone can't reach. Never throw it away.
- **Wine:** something dry and drinkable — pinot grigio, sauvignon blanc, or a dry vermouth. If you wouldn't drink it, don't cook with it.
- **Don't rinse the rice.** The surface starch is what makes risotto creamy.
- **Resting chicken** before slicing keeps it juicy when it's folded back in at the end.
- **Sous vide advantage:** chicken breast dries out easily with pan cooking alone. Sous vide at 62°C keeps it uniformly juicy — the risotto's residual heat warms it through without overcooking. A quick post-sear gives you the best of both worlds: fond for the mushrooms and moist chicken.
- **Charred asparagus (optional):** snap a bunch of asparagus, toss with olive oil and salt, and char on a griddle or hot pan while the risotto is in its final stages. Chop into 2–3cm pieces and fold in at the very end with the chicken and mushrooms. Keeps its bite and adds freshness against the rich truffle and parmesan.
