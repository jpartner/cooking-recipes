# Sourdough Step-by-Step — 500g Flour Loaf

A beginner-friendly slow-fermented overnight sourdough using Zloty Klos T750 flour. Produces a ~900g loaf with a moderately open crumb, mild tang, and chewy bite.

## Recipe at a glance

| Ingredient | Amount | Baker's % |
|---|---|---|
| Zloty Klos T750 flour | 500g | 100% |
| Water | 355g | 71% |
| Active starter (at peak) | 100g | 20% |
| Salt | 10g | 2% |

**Hold back 20g of the water for the salt addition** — use 335g for the autolyse.

## Equipment

- Large mixing bowl
- Banneton (round, ~23cm diameter)
- Dutch oven or combo cooker
- Bench scraper
- Lame or sharp blade
- Digital scale
- Heated proving box (or warm spot at ~25°C)

## Starter readiness check

Your starter is ready when it has roughly doubled, has a domed top, and passes the float test (a teaspoon dropped into water floats). Use it at peak — not before, not hours after.

## Day at a glance

| Time | Step |
|---|---|
| 8:00 AM | Feed starter |
| 1:00 PM | Autolyse |
| 2:00 PM | Mix in starter and salt |
| 2:00–6:00 PM | Bulk ferment with 3–4 folds |

## Method

### 1. Autolyse (45–60 min)

Mix 500g flour with 335g water until no dry flour remains. Cover and rest 45–60 minutes. The dough will start stiff and rough; by the end it will be smoother, more pliable, and slightly extensible. **If it feels like "bread dough" at this stage, you've added too much water.**

### 2. Mix in starter and salt

Add 100g peak starter and the remaining 20g water (with 10g salt dissolved in). Pinch and fold to incorporate fully. The dough will feel slack and sticky now — that's correct.

### 3. Bulk ferment (4–5 hours at 25°C)

Perform 3–4 sets of stretch-and-folds, every 30 minutes for the first 2 hours. After folds, leave undisturbed until the dough has risen by **about 60%**, looks pillowy, has bubbles on the surface, and jiggles when nudged.

### 4. Pre-shape (5 min) + bench rest (20–30 min)

Tip dough onto an unfloured counter. Use a bench scraper to round it into a loose ball. Rest seam-side-down, uncovered, until it relaxes outward slightly.

### 5. Final shape

Flour the top, flip, then shape into a tight boule or batard. Place seam-side-up in a well-floured banneton.

### 6. Cold retard (12–16 hours)

Cover loosely and refrigerate. **Don't fully wrap the banneton** — the surface needs to dry slightly for a good score.

### 7. Bake

Preheat Dutch oven to 250°C for at least 45 minutes. Tip dough onto parchment, score deeply with the lame, transfer to the hot pot.

- **Covered:** 20 minutes at 250°C
- **Uncovered:** 20–25 minutes at 220°C, until deeply browned

### 8. Cool

Cool on a wire rack for **at least 1 hour** before cutting. Cutting too early gives a gummy crumb.

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Likely cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Dense crumb | Under-fermented or weak starter | Longer bulk; verify starter at peak |
| Gummy crumb | Cut too early or under-baked | Wait at least 1 hour; bake longer uncovered |
| Flat loaf | Over-fermented or under-shaped | Stop bulk at 60%; tighter shaping |
| Pale crust | Oven not hot enough | Preheat longer, full 45 min minimum |
| No ear / no rise from score | Score too shallow or surface too damp | Score deeper, leave banneton uncovered in fridge |

## Notes for next time

- **Higher hydration:** try 73% (365g water) for a more open crumb.
- **Longer retard:** 24–36 hours deepens flavour and gives a darker crust.
- **Blend in rye:** swap 70g of T750 for zytnia razowa for a nuttier loaf.
- **Keep notes:** starter state, dough temperatures, total bulk time. Patterns appear faster than you'd expect.
