# Sourdough Step-by-Step — 700g Flour Loaf

The scaled-up version of the 500g recipe, sized for a 25cm banneton. Produces a ~1.25kg loaf. Same percentages, same method, slightly longer bake.

## Recipe at a glance

| Ingredient | Amount | Baker's % |
|---|---|---|
| Zloty Klos T750 flour | 700g | 100% |
| Water | 497g | 71% |
| Active starter (at peak) | 140g | 20% |
| Salt | 14g | 2% |

**Hold back 25g of the water for the salt addition** — use 472g for the autolyse.

## Scaling principles

The relationships never change:

- **Water = 71% of flour weight**
- **Starter = 20% of flour weight**
- **Salt = 2% of flour weight**

Want a different loaf size? Pick your flour weight, multiply by those percentages, done. Bulk time, fold schedule, retard, and bench rest all stay the same — they depend on temperature and inoculation, not size. **Only bake times change with larger loaves (roughly +5 minutes per 200g flour increase).**

## Method

### 1. Autolyse (45–60 min)

Mix 700g flour with 472g water until no dry flour remains. Cover and rest 45–60 minutes. Texture progression:

- **Start:** stiff, rough, almost crumbly
- **End:** smoother, pliable, slight sheen, gently stretchable

If it feels bone-dry or you can see unincorporated flour, double-check your measurements. If in doubt, do the pinch test — should hold together and feel cool and clay-like.

### 2. Mix in starter and salt

Add 140g peak starter and the remaining 25g water with 14g salt dissolved in. Pinch and fold thoroughly. The dough goes from smooth to slack and sticky here — that's correct, your mental model needs to reset at each stage.

### 3. Bulk ferment (4–5 hours at 25°C)

Three to four sets of stretch-and-folds, every 30 minutes for the first 2 hours. Then undisturbed until risen ~60%, with surface bubbles and a jiggle when nudged.

### 4. Pre-shape (5 min) + bench rest (20–30 min)

Bench-scraper round on an unfloured counter. Rest seam-down, uncovered.

### 5. Final shape

Flour the top, flip, shape into a tight boule. Seam-side-up into a 25cm well-floured banneton.

### 6. Cold retard (12–16 hours)

Cover loosely. Don't fully wrap — the surface needs to dry slightly for a clean score.

### 7. Bake

Preheat Dutch oven to 250°C for at least 45 minutes.

- **Covered:** 22 minutes at 250°C
- **Uncovered:** 25–30 minutes at 220°C until deeply browned

(Roughly +5 minutes total versus the 500g version.)

### 8. Cool

Wire rack, **minimum 90 minutes** before cutting. The bigger the loaf, the longer it needs.

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Likely cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Dense crumb | Under-fermented | Longer bulk; check starter activity |
| Gummy crumb | Under-baked or cut early | Longer uncovered bake; longer cool |
| Flat loaf | Over-fermented or weak shape | Stop bulk at 60%; tighter shaping |
| Pale crust | Oven not hot enough | Full 45+ min preheat |
| Tight crumb | Under-hydrated or under-fermented | Try 73% next bake |

## Notes for next time

- **Higher hydration:** 73% (511g water) for a more open crumb.
- **Longer retard:** 24–36 hours for deeper flavour.
- **Blend in rye:** swap ~100g T750 for zytnia razowa.
- **Watch shaping videos:** five minutes of Trevor Wilson, Full Proof Baking, or Maurizio Leo on YouTube will teach you more than any written description.
