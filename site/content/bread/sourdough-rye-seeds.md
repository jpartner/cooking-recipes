# Sourdough Step-by-Step — Rye + Seeds Variant

A 700g flour loaf with 25% whole rye and a sunflower/pumpkin seed soak. Earthier flavour, denser-but-still-open crumb, and a nuttier crust than the pure wheat version. Standalone guide — no need to cross-reference the wheat recipes.

## Recipe at a glance

### Dough

| Ingredient | Amount | Baker's % |
|---|---|---|
| Zloty Klos T750 (white wheat) | 525g | 75% |
| T2000 zytnia razowa (whole rye) | 175g | 25% |
| Water | 525g | 75% |
| Active starter (at peak) | 140g | 20% |
| Salt | 14g | 2% |

### Seed soak

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Sunflower seeds | 40g |
| Pumpkin seeds | 40g |
| Water | 80g |

**Hold back 25g of the dough water for the salt addition** — use 500g for the autolyse.

## Why the rye version works differently

- **Water absorption:** rye soaks up far more water than wheat. 75% hydration here feels similar to 71% in the all-wheat version.
- **Enzyme activity:** rye has more amylase enzymes — it ferments faster and over-ferments quickly. **Stop bulk at 50% rise, not 60%.**
- **Gluten behaviour:** rye has almost no functional gluten. Folds are about structure, not strength — be gentler.
- **Seed water theft:** unsoaked seeds will steal water from the dough during bake and give a dry crumb. Always soak.
- **Slow crumb setting:** rye crumb sets during cooling, not baking. Cut too early and it's gummy.

## Method

### 0. Seed soak (with starter feed)

When you feed your starter in the morning, also combine 40g sunflower + 40g pumpkin seeds with 80g water in a small container. Cover and leave at room temperature.

### 1. Autolyse (30–45 min)

Mix 525g T750 + 175g rye flour with 500g water until no dry flour remains. Cover and rest 30–45 minutes (shorter than the wheat version — rye is already softening rapidly).

### 2. Mix in starter, salt, and seeds

Add 140g peak starter, the remaining 25g water with 14g salt dissolved in, and the **drained soaked seeds** (discard any unabsorbed soak water). Pinch and fold to incorporate evenly.

### 3. Bulk ferment (4–4.5 hours at 25°C)

**Three** sets of stretch-and-folds at 30, 60, and 90 minutes. Be gentler than with all-wheat — rye doesn't develop in the same way.

Stop bulk when the dough has risen by **50%** (not 60%), shows surface bubbles, and jiggles. **Rye over-ferments fast — under-shoot rather than over-shoot.**

### 4. Pre-shape (5 min) + bench rest (20 min)

Light hand. Don't try to build huge tension — rye doesn't take it.

### 5. Final shape

Flour generously (rye is sticky). Shape into a boule. Seam-up into a well-floured 25cm banneton.

### 6. Cold retard (12–16 hours)

Loosely covered, fridge. Surface should dry slightly.

### 7. Bake

Preheat Dutch oven to 240°C for 45 minutes. (Lower than wheat — rye crust darkens faster.)

- **Covered:** 22 minutes at 240°C
- **Uncovered:** 25–28 minutes at 210°C, until deeply browned

### 8. Cool — properly this time

**Minimum 2 hours**, ideally overnight. This is the most important rye-specific rule. Cutting early will give you a gummy, sticky crumb regardless of how well you baked it.

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Likely cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| **Gummy crumb** | Cut too early | Always cool ≥2 hours, ideally overnight |
| Dense brick | Over-fermented (rye over-ferments fast) | Stop bulk earlier — 50% rise is enough |
| Dry crumb with hard seed bits | Seeds not soaked | Always soak seeds in 1:1 water |
| Flat, spread-out loaf | Bulk too long or shape too loose | Shorter bulk; firmer (but gentle) shape |
| Crust too dark | Bake temp too high | Drop covered phase to 230°C |

## Rye percentage scaling

Once comfortable, you can adjust the rye/wheat ratio. Approximate guidelines:

| Rye % | Hydration | Bulk rise stop | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| 10% | 72% | 60% | Barely different from pure wheat |
| 25% | 75% | 50% | This recipe — sweet spot for free-form loaves |
| 40% | 78% | 45% | Tin-baked is more reliable than free-form |
| 60%+ | 80%+ | 40% | Different method entirely — no kneading, tin-bake, expect denser results |

## Seed variations

- **Nordic-style:** 30g sunflower + 30g pumpkin + 20g flax (soaked)
- **Caraway:** 60g sunflower + 60g pumpkin + 5g caraway seeds
- **Multigrain:** 30g each of sunflower, pumpkin, sesame, and rolled oats
- **Walnut:** swap seeds for 100g toasted walnut pieces (no soak needed)
