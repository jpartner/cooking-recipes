# Fermented Chilli Sauce

A lacto-fermented hot sauce — tangy, complex, and far more interesting than anything vinegar-based. Ferment for 1–4 weeks depending on how funky you want it. Works with any combination of fresh chillies.

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Fresh chillies (mix of varieties) | 500g |
| Garlic cloves, peeled | 6–8 |
| Salt | 15g (3% of chilli + garlic weight) |
| Sugar | 1 tsp (optional — feeds fermentation) |

### For blending (after ferment)

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Ferment brine | all of it |
| Cider vinegar or white wine vinegar | 2–4 tbsp (to taste) |

## Chilli selection

Use a mix for complexity. Some starting points:

| Style | Chilli mix |
|---|---|
| All-rounder | 300g red jalapeno + 100g habanero + 100g red bell pepper |
| Smoky and hot | 250g chipotle/smoked + 150g scotch bonnet + 100g red pepper |
| Milder, fruity | 300g red bell pepper + 150g fresno + 50g habanero |
| Full send | 400g bird's eye or Thai + 100g habanero |

De-seed some or all chillies to control heat. The fermentation mellows the raw burn but doesn't reduce it dramatically.

## Method

### 1. Prep

Roughly chop the chillies and garlic. No need to be precise — everything gets blended later. Remove stems but keep seeds (unless reducing heat).

### 2. Salt and pack

Toss chillies and garlic with 15g salt in a bowl. Pack tightly into a clean jar — a 750ml–1L jar works well for this quantity. Press down firmly so the chillies release liquid and sit below the brine. If they don't submerge after a few hours, top up with a 3% salt water solution (15g salt per 500ml water).

### 3. Weight and cover

Place a weight on top to keep everything submerged — a small ziplock bag filled with brine works well, or a glass weight. Cover loosely with a cloth or use an airlock lid if you have one.

### 4. Ferment (1–4 weeks at room temperature)

| Duration | Result |
|---|---|
| 1 week | Mild tang, still tastes quite fresh |
| 2 weeks | Good balance of tang and chilli flavour — a solid stopping point |
| 3–4 weeks | Deeper, funkier, more complex — worth the wait |

Burp the jar daily for the first week (open lid to release CO2). Taste from week 1 — stop when you like the flavour. Bubbling should be active in the first week, then slow down.

**Signs of a healthy ferment:** bubbles, sour smell, cloudy brine, tangy taste. **Signs of trouble:** mould on surface (scrape off if minor, discard if extensive), foul smell (not sour — foul).

### 5. Blend

Pour everything — chillies, garlic, and all the brine — into a blender or food processor. Add 2–4 tbsp vinegar. Blend until smooth.

For a thinner sauce, add more brine or a splash of water. For a thicker, chunkier sauce, pulse rather than blend.

### 6. Strain (optional)

For a smooth, pourable hot sauce, pass through a fine sieve. Press with a spoon to extract as much liquid as possible. The leftover pulp is excellent mixed into marinades or stir-fries.

Skip straining for a thicker, more rustic sauce.

### 7. Bottle

Transfer to clean bottles or jars. Refrigerate.

## Notes

- **3% salt is the target.** Weigh the chillies and garlic together, multiply by 0.03. Too little salt risks bad bacteria; too much slows fermentation.
- **Wear gloves** when handling hot chillies — especially habaneros and scotch bonnets. Don't touch your face.
- **Shelf life:** 6+ months refrigerated. The vinegar and low pH keep it stable. It continues to develop flavour slowly in the fridge.
- **Scaling:** scales linearly. For a bigger batch, use a larger jar and maintain the 3% salt ratio.
- **Garlic turns blue/green** sometimes during fermentation — this is harmless. It's a reaction between compounds in the garlic and the acidic environment.
