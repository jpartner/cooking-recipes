# Kimchi — 4.5kg Cabbage Batch

Scaled for 6 napa cabbages (~4.5kg total). Punchy, properly spicy version with both gochugaru and gochujang.

## Salt soak

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Napa cabbage | ~4.5kg (6 cabbages) |
| Coarse sea salt | 450g (~10% of cabbage weight) |

Quarter the cabbages, layer salt generously between leaves (concentrating on the thick white parts), then cover with water. Soak **6–8 hours or overnight**, flipping halfway. The cabbage is ready when it bends without snapping.

After soaking, **rinse 3 times thoroughly** and drain well — give it 1–2 hours to drip dry.

## Kimchi paste

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Gochugaru (Korean chilli flakes) | 300–350g (3–3.5 cups) |
| Gochujang | 3–4 tbsp |
| Fish sauce | 120ml |
| Saeujeot (salted shrimp) | 80g |
| Garlic, minced | 100g (~20–24 cloves) |
| Ginger, minced | 50g |
| Sugar | 2–3 tbsp |
| Sweet rice flour | 3 tbsp |
| Water (for rice flour paste) | 300ml |

**Rice flour paste:** combine sweet rice flour with 300ml water in a small saucepan, cook over low heat, stirring, until thick and glossy. Cool fully before mixing.

Combine the cooled rice flour paste with all other paste ingredients. The gochujang adds depth, umami, and a fermented funk that rounds out the heat — it also helps the paste stick to the leaves.

## Vegetables to mix in

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Daikon radish, julienned | 500–600g |
| Spring onions, 3–4cm pieces | 2–3 bunches |
| Carrots, julienned (optional) | 1–2 |

## Method

1. **Salt soak** (above) — 6–8 hours or overnight, flipping halfway.

2. **Rinse and drain** — 3 thorough rinses, then 1–2 hours dripping dry.

3. **Make the paste** — cook and cool the rice flour paste, then combine with all other paste ingredients.

4. **Mix paste with vegetables** — fold the daikon, spring onions, and optional carrots into the paste.

5. **Coat the cabbage** — wearing gloves, work the paste between every leaf of every quarter. Be thorough; the leaves take more paste than you'd think at this batch size.

6. **Pack** — tightly into clean containers (fermentation jars or food-grade containers with weights). Press down to remove air pockets. Leave at least 5cm headspace.

7. **Room-temp ferment** — 1–2 days at room temperature, until you see bubbles forming and taste a slight tang.

8. **Refrigerate** — slows fermentation dramatically. Continues developing flavour over weeks.

## Notes

- **Better to have extra paste than not enough** — at this batch size you really can't have too much.
- **6 cabbages at 4.5kg total are on the smaller side.** Adjust quantities up if your cabbages are larger — salt and paste both scale linearly.
- **First few days at room temp:** open the container daily to release pressure and press the kimchi back down under its brine.
- **Eating window:** best from week 2 to month 3, but lasts 6+ months refrigerated and gets funkier over time.
- **Save some paste:** great for kimchi fried rice, marinades, or boosting the next batch.
